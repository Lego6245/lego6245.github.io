Dear Rioters,

        I initially learned about the power of programing way back in the third grade, on old iMacs using Stagecast Creator, working with students who were almost twice my age! Since then, the tools may have changed, but the passion that got me into an eight grade class as a third grader only continued to grow, to where I am releasing mobile apps on the play store and building cool websites at hackathons.

        

My passion for gaming grew along side my skill as a developer. I picked up Team Fortress 2 over one thousand hours of gameplay ago, and since have played it competitively in UGC and casted many a match with eXtelevision. Games like Super Mario Sunshine, Legend of Zelda: the Wind Waker, and Golden Sun had me exploring new worlds and conquering new challenges. Soon, I would discover League of Legends, and I would pick it up solidly around the start of the first LCS last year. Now, working my way up from Silver, I’ve poured many hours into working on my mechanics and having fun with my friends along the way.

        

This love for League played into the creation of WardScore. WardScore uses PHP and the Riot Games API to discover players warding habits and presents them with a WardScore as a representation of their overall warding prowess. With about one hundred thousand summoners in our database and with almost seven and a half million wards tracked, we were ecstatic with the response to our site, but we know there is so much more that can be done as the API develops and as more people visit WardScore.

        

One hundred thousand is a small number when compared to the League of Legends player base. The websites that represent League are visited by multiples of hundreds of thousands of prospective and current players every day. To make the web experiences as magical as possible, you need a special sort of person, and I feel I’ve got the best background to do it. From experience working on both sides of an API at the Office of the President and building out cool web experiences like WardScore, to my passion for gaming and development, I feel as though Riot Games would be the perfect environment to truly flex my abilities, and to create something that leaves a mark like a Malphite Ground Slam.  

Yours,

William Saulnier
